Quick Decision Guide

  • Payer already recouped? → Close via 108259, document, complete
  • Reason is vague? → Dispute in Availity or Contest by paper
  • Refund is valid? → Retraction if retracting payer; mail check otherwise
  • Refund NOT owed? → Deny with letter, EOB/COB support
  • Post-Paid Audit flagged? → Do NOT update correspondence; use 1081092

Payer Quick Reference

  • BCBS / Florida Blue → Retracting · 40-day window
  • Sunshine / Ambetter → Always retracting · NEVER issue check · 30-day dispute
  • Humana Medicaid → Verify COB stack · Medicaid is always last
  • Non-retracting payers → Mail refund check with worksheet
  • Medicare → Primary: 65+, retired, ESRD, employer <100 emp.
⚠️ Critical Sequencing Rule Always update correspondence (AC + 144) BEFORE posting recoupment code 16.1041. Posting recoupment first can reverse the recoupment line.
⚠️ Rejected Bucket Rule Only recoupment postings (16.1041) and refund postings are allowed in the Rejected/Replacement Bucket. Reverse all other adjustments.
📅 Timeliness Rule Always use the Deposit Date — not Posting Date — when calculating the 30-month statutory lookback. Posting dates can change with transfers.
Terminology
Complete definitions for all terms used in the refund process.
Refund Request (RR)
A payer's formal written or electronic notice requesting UF Health return an overpayment. This is a solicited refund.
Refund Retraction (Approve)
UF agrees with the refund request and instructs the payer to recoup/offset from future claims. No check is mailed.
Retracting Payer
A payer that recoups funds directly from future claims rather than accepting a mailed check. Examples: Sunshine Health, Ambetter, many BCBS lines.
Contest
UF requests additional detail from the payer because the stated reason is generic or insufficient. Used for paper letters. Equivalent to "Dispute" in Availity.
Dispute
The Availity portal action equivalent to a Contest letter. Selected when payer's reason is not specific.
Denial
UF does not agree with the payer's reason or requested amount. A denial letter is sent explaining why no refund is due.
Recoupment
When a payer takes money back from future claim payments without prior notice or after retraction approval.
Offset
Payer deducts the overpayment from a future claim payment. Same end result as recoupment.
Post-Paid Audit — Refund Requested
Status indicating the refund has moved to departmental review (e.g., medical records, coding). Do NOT update correspondence. Add 1081092 and defer.
HAR
Hospital Account Record. Each patient visit has a unique HAR. All refund work is performed at the HAR level.
Series Account
Multiple dates of service billed under the same HAR (e.g., infusion therapy series). Always identify the exact DOS targeted by the refund request.
Claim Number / Payer Control Number
The payer's claim identifier. The label varies by portal or EOB. Used in all correspondence and Epic notes.
Invoice Number
Internal payer identifier that appears on refund letters. Must be included in all letters, notes, and Availity actions.
Liability Bucket
Epic screen where insurance and patient balances are managed.
Rejected / Replacement Bucket
Epic bucket where reversed/rebilled lines are held. Only recoupment postings and refund postings are permitted here.
Re-Evaluate Expected Reimbursement
Bucket Activity #8 in Epic. Recomputes expected contract rates using current 3M/CGS data. Must be run before validating any recalculation or CPT-level refund request.
Deposit Date vs Posting Date
For timeliness rules, always use the Deposit Date. Posting dates can change when accounts are moved or transferred.
Deferral
Temporarily pausing work on a refund account with a documented follow-up date and documented reason.
Availity
The web portal used by BCBS for refund request management, disputes, and resolutions.
OnBase
Document management system where scanned refund request letters are stored. Search by patient name, account #, or claim #.
Epic
The primary system used to review accounts, payment history, and post all refund actions.
30-Month Timeliness Rule
Payer refund requests must be submitted within 30 months of the deposit date (Florida Statute §627.6131). Requests outside this window may be denied on timeliness grounds.
NRP (Next Responsible Party)
The next payer in line after a refund or recoupment — either another insurance or the patient (self-pay). Do not create credits; transfer the balance to NRP properly.
COB (Coordination of Benefits)
Rules governing which insurance pays first, second, or last when a patient has multiple insurance plans.
EDM (Enterprise Denial Management)
Department that handles complex coding-related denial management. NOT responsible for inpatient coding, DRG, NDC billing, medical records, or policy-driven refunds.
RI (Revenue Integrity)
Department that validates proper charging. Route inpatient audit refunds, drug/unit issues, and overpayment under medical and pharmacy benefits to RI.
CDI (Clinical Documentation Improvement)
Route inpatient diagnosis and DRG coding issues to CDI. EDM does not perform DRG coding.
HIM (Health Information Management)
Route inpatient discharge status update requests to HIM. EDM does not update discharge status.
MSP (Medicare Secondary Payer)
Federal rules determining when Medicare is the primary versus secondary payer. Key factors: patient age, retirement status, employer size, ESRD/disability status.
EOB (Explanation of Benefits)
The document sent by an insurer detailing how a claim was processed, including allowed amounts, patient responsibility, and denied charges.
DOS (Date of Service)
The date the medical service was rendered. Must always be verified against the refund request and Epic records.
CPT (Current Procedural Terminology)
Standardized codes used to describe medical procedures and services. Payers may request refunds at the CPT line level.
DRG (Diagnosis-Related Group)
A classification system used for Medicare inpatient hospital payments. DRG audits and coding disputes are routed to CDI/RI, not EDM.
NDC (National Drug Code)
Unique identifier for drugs. Missing or incorrect NDC on claims is a PFS billing issue, not EDM.
Refund Workflow Lanes
Select the correct lane based on the payer type and refund scenario.
Lane A — Retracting Payer (BCBS / Sunshine / Ambetter)
  1. Validate reason and amount — run Re-Evaluate Expected Reimbursement if recalculation or CPT-level.
  2. Enter 108193 — Overpayment Calculation (line-by-line).
  3. Enter 108455 — Refund Retraction Approved.
  4. Update Correspondence → Status: AC · Reason: 144 (Retraction Approved).
  5. Post recoupment 16.1041 in Bucket #6 only. ⚠️ Correspondence must be updated FIRST.
  6. Create Informational Letter via 108484 — telling payer to recoup (no check).
  7. Print: Refund Request pages first → then Letter PDF.
  8. Verify buckets are clean (no red, no stray adjustments). Mark complete.
Lane B — Insufficient/Vague Reason ("provider billing error" etc.)
  1. In Availity → Dispute Overpayment. Select: "Reason is not specific on the refund letter."
  2. Paste standard comment: "Please provide detailed overpayment explanation including allowance, patient responsibility, and any denied/non-covered charges."
  3. Submit. (Invoice turns black → yellow in Availity.)
  4. Defer in Epic using 1058 (30 days).
  5. Enter 108219 in Epic — Availity Refund Dispute logged.
  6. Update Correspondence. Attach EOB after dispute is filed (portal restriction).
  7. Follow up on or before deferral date. If payer provides valid reason, proceed to retraction or denial lane.
Lane C — Coverage Terminated / COB Incorrect
  1. Enter 108225 — Coverage Change (document payer + policy # + reason + DOS + "initiate billing").
  2. Enter 108226 — Claim Billing Change ("Billing [Payer] as primary/secondary").
  3. Undo Billing — strict order: Self-Pay first, then insurance lines bottom to top.
  4. Reverse any adjustments in Rejected bucket (except recoupments/refunds).
  5. Update Filing Order → Initiate Billing → verify insurance name matches → Accept.
  6. Enter 108193 — Overpayment Calculation.
  7. If payer is retracting: 108455 + post 16.1041 in Bucket #6.
  8. Create letter via 108484 → Print Refund Request first → then Letter PDF.
  9. Update Correspondence (AC + 144). Mark complete.
Lane D — Departmental Ownership (Post-Paid Audit)
  1. If Follow-Up shows "Post-Paid Audit — Refund Requested": Do NOT update correspondence.
  2. Enter 1081092 — Refund Request Reviewed, Not Resolved.
  3. Add note: "Pending department review and response."
  4. Defer using 1058 or 1059 as appropriate.
  5. Monitor. Do not guess amounts. Wait for department to create credit entry before proceeding.
Lane E — COB Accounts (Secondary Payer Overpayment)
  1. Verify filing order and coverage stack in Epic. Confirm which payer is primary and which is secondary.
  2. Pull both EOBs — primary and secondary. Confirm the secondary allowance, primary payment, and secondary payment amounts.
  3. Run COB Calculation: Secondary Allowance − Primary Paid = Secondary Responsibility. If Secondary Paid > Secondary Responsibility → overpayment exists. Secondary payers have no contract — they pay up to their allowance minus what primary paid, nothing more.
  4. For unsolicited credits: use expected reimbursement to determine if the credit is from a payment or an adjustment before proceeding.
  5. Enter 108225 — Coverage Change if registration updates are needed.
  6. Enter 108226 — Claim Billing Change if rebilling is required.
  7. Enter 1081093 — Overpayment Calculation using the COB long-form wording:
    "Refunding [Payer] $[X] as secondary. [Payer] is responsible to pay up to their allowance $[A], minus the primary insurance payment $[P] = $[A−P] as their secondary responsibility. [Payer] paid $[Y], overpaid $[X]."
  8. If medical records were sent with the request → you MUST send a contest letter (108483) OR issue a final denial (108484). Do not close without one of these actions.
  9. If the refund is valid and payer is retracting: enter 108455 → post 16.1041 in Bucket #6.
  10. If mailing a refund check: complete refund worksheet (see Refund Worksheet section). Short-form bucket note: "[PAYER] PAID MORE THAN ALLOWABLE AS SECONDARY. ISSUED REFUND. EOB(s) ATTACHED."
  11. Update Correspondence → Status: AC · Reason: 144. Print and file.
  12. ⚠️ If secondary payment is at or below calculated allowance: verify payer's basis before closing. Do not mark "no overpayment" — secondary payers have no contract. Confirm with contest or denial.

How Payment Varies Per Case

          
Each case gets assigned an MS-DRG at discharge based on: principal diagnosis · secondary diagnoses (CCs/MCCs) · procedures performed · age · sex · discharge status. The MS-DRG has a relative weight — higher weight = more resources = higher payment. That weight is multiplied by the adjusted standardized amount to produce the base payment. Additional adjustments are added for: DSH (safety net hospitals) · IME (teaching hospitals) · Outliers (unusually costly cases).

        

        

          Web Pricer Access
          The CMS IPPS Web Pricer (cms.gov/ipps-webpricer) allows you to enter a DRG, wage index, and hospital information to get the exact expected Medicare payment for FYs 2020–2026. Use this to verify expected allowed amounts for Medicare inpatient claims when payer requests a refund.
        

      


      
      

        

          
What is a DRG?

          
A Diagnosis-Related Group (MS-DRG) is the classification Medicare uses to pay for an inpatient stay. Instead of paying for each individual service, Medicare pays one flat rate per discharge based on what DRG the case falls into. There are 772 MS-DRGs in FY 2026.

        

        

          
1
Patient Discharged
Diagnosis codes (ICD-10-CM), procedure codes (ICD-10-PCS), age, sex, and discharge status are finalized on the claim.

          
2
MS-DRG Assigned by Grouper
The CMS MS-DRG Grouper software (V43 for FY 2026) assigns the case to an MS-DRG. CCs (complications) and MCCs (major complications) can move a case to a higher-paying DRG.

          
3
Relative Weight Applied
Each DRG has a relative weight. DRG 001 (heart transplant) has a very high weight (~25); DRG 795 (normal newborn) has a very low weight (~0.17). Weights are recalibrated annually.

          
4
Adjusted Standardized Amount
FY 2026 base: $6,752.61 (operating) + $524.15 (capital). The labor portion (66%) is adjusted by the local wage index. The non-labor portion (34%) stays flat (except AK/HI).

          
5
Add-On Adjustments
DSH (disproportionate share), IME (teaching hospital indirect medical education), and Outlier payments are added if applicable. These can significantly increase the base payment.

          
6
Final Payment Issued
Medicare pays this single amount as payment-in-full. The hospital cannot bill separately for anything included in the DRG bundle (other than physician professional fees under the PFS).

        

        

          Refund Implication
          When a payer claims overpayment on an inpatient Medicare case, the expected allowed = the DRG-based payment, not individual charge amounts. Always use Epic's Re-Evaluate Expected Reimbursement to get the contract/expected rate, and use the CMS Web Pricer to verify Medicare amounts.

Rejected Buckets — Rules & Handling

      
Understanding when rejected buckets appear and how to resolve them correctly.


      
What is a Rejected Bucket?
A rejected bucket occurs when undo billing is processed. Any payments posted before the undo billing will appear as a credit. Rejected buckets are created whenever undo billing is performed — e.g., when late charges appear or new insurance plans are added. This is NOT the result of all charges being removed.


      

        
Primary Billing Error
Bucket is rejected because the wrong insurance was billed as primary. Undo billing was performed and the correct insurance was re-billed as primary.

        
Rejected Red Bucket — No Balance
Occurs when there is an open correspondence or follow-up record associated with the bucket, indicating pending action or review.

        
Secondary Rejected Bucket
When undo billing is performed on a secondary bucket, charges move to the primary bucket. The Patient Responsibility (PTR) or balance was previously sent to the Next Responsible Party (NRP) when responsibility shifted.

      


      

        
Handling Credits on Rejected Buckets

        
If credit results from a payment posted before undo billing:

        
    
          
  • Check if coverage changed — is the payer now secondary?
    • 
          
  • If secondary: determine if payment can be transferred based on payment/follow-up status of the primary bucket
    • 
          
  • If transfer is appropriate: move payment from rejected bucket or defer as needed
    • 
          
  • If secondary bucket not showing: transfer PTR/balance to NRP (secondary payer if applicable)
    • 
          
  • Sometimes transfer is automatic; if not, transfer manually
    • 
        

        
If credit results from an adjustment (not a payment):

        
    
          
  • Reverse the adjustment — Reason: Other
    • 
          
  • Comment: "Reversed adj on the rejected inv line to decrease/resolve credit."
    • 
          
  • Never perform a contractual adjustment on a rejected bucket to correct a credit
    • 
        

        
Exception: If the credit is eligible for an unsolicited refund (meets unsolicited criteria), the credit may be processed as a refund instead.

Medicaid COB Calculations

      
How to calculate Medicaid's secondary responsibility. Source: Training — Traditional Medicaid Secondary.


      

        
Medicaid Secondary — Commercial/Managed Care Primary

        
Medicaid pays the lesser of:

        

          
Option A: The co-insurance amount applied to PTR by the primary insurance

          
Option B: Medicaid's allowance minus the primary insurance payment

        

        
Steps:

        
    
          
  1. What is Medicaid's allowance?
    2. 
          
  2. Subtract primary insurance payment → this is the most Medicaid would pay (Option B)
    3. 
          
  3. What amount did primary apply to PTR? (Option A)
    4. 
          
  4. Compare Option A and Option B
    5. 
          
  5. Medicaid is responsible for the lesser amount
    6. 
        

      


      

        
Example Table — Medicaid Allowance: $300.00

        

          
Primary PaidPTRMedicaid ResponsibilityExplanation

          
            
$500.00$60.00$0.00Primary paid more than Medicaid allowance ($300). No secondary responsibility. Adjust PTR on Medicaid line using 133000.

            
$200.00$25.00$25.00Medicaid pays the PTR ($25) because it's less than the most Medicaid would pay ($300 − $200 = $100). Pays the co-insurance up to their allowance.

            
$100.00$300.00$200.00Most Medicaid would pay = $300 − $100 = $200. PTR of $300 exceeds this. Medicaid pays $200; remaining $100 adjusted using 133000.

          
        

        
Note: This secondary reimbursement does NOT apply to claims where Auto is primary.

Overpayment Calculation Format — Required Standards

      
Standardized format required for all accounts effective 2-4-2020. Activity code 1081093 — HBCR Overpayment Calculations.


      
Key Rules · For payers NOT in Availity, use Claim Number in place of Invoice Number. · "No Calculations Needed" applies only to COB issues and coverage termed — but must still include the COB rule or termination date. · Always explain any remaining debit or credit balance after a refund or retraction.


      

        Unsolicited Refunds — Expected Reimbursement: When working unsolicited refunds, use the expected reimbursement to determine whether the credit is the result of a payment or an adjustment. This determines the correct path for the refund.
      


      

        
COB Secondary — Note Wording Examples

        
Use this format for secondary payer COB calculations in your 1081093 note. The payer name, allowance, primary payment, secondary responsibility, amount paid, and overpaid amount must all be stated clearly.


        

          
Full Calculation Wording (Detailed)

          

          
Refunding [Payer] $[overpaid amt] as secondary. [Payer] is responsible to pay up to their allowance $[allowance], minus the primary insurance payment $[primary pmt] = $[secondary resp] as their secondary responsibility. [Payer] paid $[amount paid], overpaid $[overpaid amt].

(Payer may request the $[difference] be applied to the PT)

        


        

          
Worked Example — Cigna Secondary

          

          
Refunding Cigna $1,252.89 as secondary. Cigna is responsible to pay up to their allowance $6,271.95, minus the primary insurance payment $1,302.89 = $4,969.06 as their secondary responsibility. Cigna paid $6,221.95, overpaid $1,252.89.

(Payer may request the $50.00 be applied to the PT)

        


        

          
Short Form Wording (Account Note / Bucket Note)

          

          
[PAYER] PAID MORE THAN ALLOWABLE AS SECONDARY. ISSUED REFUND. EOB(s) ATTACHED. TX [#], INV [#].

        


        

          Note: The difference between what the payer should have paid (secondary responsibility) and what they paid (overpaid amount) may sometimes result in a small remaining balance. Always note if the payer may request that balance be applied to the patient.
